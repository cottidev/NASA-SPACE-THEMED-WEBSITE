April 10, 2026 · Astronomy Picture of the Day

Stellar Nursery
in Eagle Nebula

The iconic Pillars of Creation, imaged here by the James Webb Space Telescope in near-infrared light, reveal newborn stars emerging from dense columns of gas and dust. The tallest pillar stretches approximately four light-years — roughly the distance from our Sun to the nearest star system. This region, located 6,500 light-years away in the Eagle Nebula (M16), continues to be one of the most studied stellar nurseries in our galaxy.
